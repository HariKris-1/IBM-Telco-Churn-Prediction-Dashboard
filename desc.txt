ALGORITHM & METHOD DESCRIPTIONS
================================

Chi-Square Test of Independence
-------------------------------
The chi-square test checks whether two categorical variables are related
or independent. In this project it tests whether a customer's Contract
type (month-to-month, one-year, two-year) is associated with whether
they churn. A small p-value (< 0.05) means the churn rate genuinely
differs across contract types and the pattern is unlikely due to chance.

Independent Two-Sample T-Test
-----------------------------
The t-test compares the average of a numeric variable between two groups
to see if the difference is statistically meaningful. Here it compares
average Monthly Charges for customers who churned versus those who stayed.
If the p-value is below 0.05, the price difference between the two groups
is real, not random noise.

Two-Proportion Z-Test
---------------------
The z-test for proportions compares two success rates to decide whether
they differ significantly. In the simulated A/B test section it compares
the retention rate of a control group (no offer) against a treatment group
(retention offer). A significant result means the offer genuinely changed
the retention rate beyond what chance would explain.

Power Analysis / Sample Size Estimation
---------------------------------------
Power analysis calculates how many observations you need to reliably
detect a real effect of a given size. In this project it estimates the
minimum number of customers per A/B-test group needed to detect the
difference between a 5% and 15% retention rate with 80% probability
(power) at a 5% significance level, using Cohen's h as the effect size.

Logistic Regression
-------------------
Logistic Regression is a linear model that predicts the probability of a
binary outcome — here, whether a customer will churn. Features are
standardised (zero mean, unit variance) so that coefficients are
comparable and the optimiser converges reliably. It serves as a fast,
interpretable baseline before trying more complex models.

Random Forest Classifier
-------------------------
Random Forest builds many decision trees on random subsets of the data
and averages their predictions, which reduces overfitting compared to a
single tree. In this project 200 trees are trained to predict churn, and
the model's feature-importance scores reveal which customer attributes
(e.g. tenure, contract type) drive predictions most strongly.

ROC-AUC (Receiver Operating Characteristic — Area Under the Curve)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
ROC-AUC measures how well a classifier distinguishes between the two
classes across all possible decision thresholds. A score of 0.5 means
random guessing; 1.0 means perfect separation. Both the Logistic
Regression and Random Forest models are evaluated with ROC-AUC so their
discriminative ability can be compared on a single, threshold-independent
metric.
